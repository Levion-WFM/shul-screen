# How the Shul Screen Works

**A quick guide for the person who keeps it running.**

---

## What is this?

The TV in the shul shows the day's zmanim, the weekly schedule, Shabbos times,
announcements, and who sponsored the kiddush. It updates itself — you don't
need to reboot it, turn it on, or do anything with the TV. A small computer
behind the TV keeps it running day and night.

## What updates on its own (you never touch these)

- **The clock** in the top-right corner.
- **Today's Hebrew date** in the top-left.
- **Daily zmanim** (alos, sunrise, sof zman shema, shkiyah, tzeis, etc.).
- **Sefiras Ha'Omer** in the bottom strip during Sefirah season.
- **The "Next" countdown** at the bottom of the zmanim list.
- **Which Shabbos is being shown.** Every Saturday night at midnight, the
  card on the right automatically switches to the *next* Shabbos.

If any of those look wrong, don't try to fix them on the screen — call whoever
set it up.

## What you can change

Open a web browser on your phone or computer and go to:

> **_[your shul's backend website — ask whoever set this up]_**

You'll see a page with boxes for each section of the display. Type what you
want, then press **Save** at the bottom. The TV updates within about 30
seconds.

What you can edit:

- **Shabbos times** — candle lighting, mincha, shacharis, havdalah, parsha.
  Whatever you type here applies to *this coming Shabbos only*. Next week
  goes back to whatever was pre-entered in the system.
- **Weekly schedule** — the davening times on the left side.
- **Shiurim** — names and times of regular shiurim.
- **Seder limud** — kollel boker, seder layla, etc.
- **Sponsors** — the brass plaque on the bottom right (kiddush, ner l'maor, etc.).
- **Announcements & fliers** — the pictures/text in the middle column.
- **Community names** — yahrzeits, aufrufs, etc.
- **Welcome message / reminder** — the text in the gold strip along the bottom.
- **Zecher nishmas** — the small dedication in the bottom-right corner.

## If the screen looks frozen or wrong

1. **Wait a minute.** The screen refreshes every 30 seconds. Sometimes the
   internet hiccups and it catches up on its own.
2. **Unplug the small black box behind the TV for 10 seconds, then plug it
   back in.** Wait about 1 minute — the screen will come back by itself.
   (Don't unplug the TV — only the small box.)
3. **Still wrong?** Call whoever set this up. Don't press buttons on the TV
   remote; you can't break anything from the backend website, but the TV's
   own settings can get the picture into a weird state.

## One thing to remember

Shabbos times roll over to next week automatically after Motzei Shabbos. If
you manually edit Shabbos times in the backend, your edit only affects *that
one week* — next week's times will show whatever was pre-loaded. That's by
design.
